# DOFH #001
## The Portal

The Junior Officer burst into the room.

"Sir, the portal is down."

The Digital Officer From Hell looked up from his tea.

"Which portal?"

"The National Unified Citizen Service Delivery and Digital Empowerment Portal."

The Officer frowned.

"That's a terrible name."

"Yes sir."

"Has anybody used it?"

"Millions."

The Officer sighed.

"That explains the outage."

The Junior Officer hesitated.

"Sir, citizens can't access essential services."

"Excellent."

"Excellent?"

"If citizens can access services whenever they want, they develop expectations."

The Junior Officer looked confused.

"Shouldn't services be available?"

The Officer laughed.

"Young man, availability is a feature. Expectations are a liability."

"Should we issue a statement?"

"Of course."

The Officer opened a document titled Standard Portal Incident Response Template.

He read aloud.

"The portal is experiencing temporary technical difficulties due to unprecedented user enthusiasm."

"But sir, the database server caught fire."

"Exactly."

"How is that user enthusiasm?"

"If nobody used it, the server wouldn't have been working."

The Junior Officer nodded slowly.

Years of service had already begun to take effect.
