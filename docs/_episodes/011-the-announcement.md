# DOFH #011

## The Announcement

The Junior Officer entered carrying a presentation deck.

It was thick enough to be classified as a structural element.

"Sir, the Transformation Programme has been approved."

The Digital Officer From Hell looked up from his tea.

"The entire programme?"

"Yes."

"Already?"

"The announcement is tomorrow."

The Officer nodded.

"Excellent."

The Junior Officer opened the presentation.

Slide One.

DIGITAL TRANSFORMATION 2030

Slide Two.

REIMAGINING THE FUTURE

Slide Three.

EMPOWERING STAKEHOLDERS

Slide Four.

DIGITAL-FIRST EXPERIENCE

The Officer smiled.

"This is good."

"Really?"

"Very good."

The Junior Officer looked relieved.

"Because the architecture isn't complete."

"Good."

"The requirements are still changing."

"Good."

"The vendor hasn't been selected."

"Good."

"The system hasn't been built."

"Excellent."

The Junior Officer hesitated.

"Sir, shouldn't some of those things exist before the announcement?"

The Officer looked confused.

"Why?"

"So we know what we're announcing."

The Officer put down his tea.

"Young man."

"Yes sir."

"Announcements create momentum."

"Do they?"

"Absolutely."

"What if the system isn't ready?"

The Officer shrugged.

"That is an implementation detail."

The Junior Officer looked at the deployment schedule.

"Sir, the programme affects millions of users."

The Officer nodded.

"Excellent."

"The timeline is extremely aggressive."

"Excellent."

"The budget appears optimistic."

"Excellent."

"The risks haven't been assessed."

The Officer smiled.

"Now you're understanding transformation."

The Junior Officer wasn't sure he was.

The Officer stood and walked to the window.

The city stretched to the horizon.

Thousands of offices.

Thousands of systems.

Thousands of users.

Every one of them waiting for digital transformation.

"Tell me," said the Officer.

"What?"

"What is the most important part of any transformation programme?"

The Junior Officer thought carefully.

"The technology?"

"No."

"The planning?"

"No."

"The execution?"

"No."

The Officer pointed at the presentation.

"The logo."

"The logo?"

"The logo."

The Junior Officer looked at Slide One again.

A consultant had clearly spent weeks designing it.

"It is quite nice."

"Exactly."

The Officer smiled.

"The logo is complete."

"But the system isn't."

The Officer nodded.

"Yet one of them can be shown at tomorrow's press conference."

The Junior Officer made a note.

It seemed important.

The Officer returned to his desk.

"Has the media been invited?"

"Yes."

"Excellent."

"Should we invite the technical team?"

The Officer looked horrified.

"Certainly not."

"Why?"

"They might answer questions."

The Junior Officer closed his notebook.

The transformation had officially begun.

Nothing existed.

Everything was on schedule.
