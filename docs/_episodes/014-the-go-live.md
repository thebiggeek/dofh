# DOFH #014

## The Go-Live

Launch day arrived.

The atmosphere was electric.

Banners had been printed.

Press releases had been prepared.

Speeches had been rehearsed.

The system had not.

The Junior Officer entered the control room.

"Sir."

The Digital Officer From Hell looked up.

"Yes?"

"The infrastructure team has concerns."

"Excellent."

"The application team has concerns."

"Excellent."

"The security team has concerns."

"Excellent."

"The vendor has concerns."

The Officer smiled.

"Now everyone is aligned."

The Junior Officer looked at the dashboard.

Several indicators were already flashing.

"Should we postpone?"

The Officer looked offended.

"Postpone?"

"The system isn't ready."

The Officer pointed at the schedule.

"What does it say?"

The Junior Officer read it.

GO-LIVE

09:00

"Exactly."

"But the system isn't ready."

The Officer looked confused.

"What does readiness have to do with scheduling?"

The countdown reached zero.

At exactly nine o'clock, the launch ceremony began.

Applause filled the room.

The portal immediately stopped responding.

The Junior Officer stared at the screen.

"Sir."

"Yes?"

"The portal is down."

The Officer looked at the stage.

The speeches continued.

"The launch appears successful."

"The system is unavailable."

"The ceremony is available."

The Junior Officer checked the monitoring dashboard.

Error rates were rising.

Response times were rising.

Support tickets were rising.

Everything else was rising.

Except availability.

"Should we stop the launch?"

The Officer looked shocked.

"During the launch?"

The Junior Officer nodded.

The Officer shook his head.

"That would create confusion."

The Junior Officer stared at the screen.

"I believe the outage is already creating confusion."

The Officer ignored him.

The keynote speech concluded.

Photographs were taken.

Announcements were issued.

The transformation programme was officially declared operational.

At that precise moment the system crashed completely.

The control room fell silent.

The Junior Officer looked defeated.

"Sir, the entire platform is unavailable."

The Officer nodded.

"Excellent."

"Excellent?"

"The transformation has begun."

Hours later an official statement was released.

'The launch has been overwhelmingly successful and user response has exceeded expectations.'

The Junior Officer read it twice.

"Exceeded expectations?"

The Officer smiled.

"Far exceeded."

"How do we know?"

The Officer pointed at the outage graph.

The line had reached levels never previously observed.

The Officer nodded approvingly.

"That level of engagement was completely unexpected."
