# DOFH #021

## The Certifications

The Audit began with a simple question.

The Junior Officer entered carrying three binders.

"Sir."

The Digital Officer From Hell looked up.

"What now?"

"The auditors would like to see the certifications."

The Officer smiled.

"Excellent."

The Junior Officer placed the binders on the desk.

Each was several hundred pages thick.

Each contained certificates.

Each certified something.

The problem was that nobody knew what.

The auditors arrived.

They opened the first binder.

"This certificate confirms compliance."

"Excellent."

The auditors nodded.

"Compliance with what?"

The room became silent.

The Vendor checked his notes.

The Consultants checked their notes.

The Programme Office checked its notes.

Nobody could find the answer.

The auditors opened the second binder.

"This certificate confirms security."

"Excellent."

"Security of what?"

More silence.

The Vendor located the certificate.

It referred to a system.

The system was not the system currently under discussion.

The auditors opened the third binder.

"This certification expired two years ago."

The Officer looked puzzled.

"It was valid when it was issued."

The auditors stared.

The Junior Officer buried his face in his hands.

Weeks later the findings arrived.

The project possessed:

* Compliance Certificates
* Security Certificates
* Readiness Certificates
* Completion Certificates

The auditors were unable to determine whether any of them applied to the project.

The Officer remained optimistic.

"At least we have certificates."

The Junior Officer looked exhausted.

"What exactly do they prove?"

The Officer smiled.

"That somebody certified something."
