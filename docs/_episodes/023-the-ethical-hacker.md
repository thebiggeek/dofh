# DOFH #023

## The Ethical Hacker

The first vulnerability report arrived on a Monday.

The second arrived on Tuesday.

The third arrived on Wednesday.

The Junior Officer entered carrying a printed email.

"Sir."

The Digital Officer From Hell looked up.

"We have a vulnerability report."

The Officer nodded.

"Excellent."

"The reporter claims there is a serious issue."

"Excellent."

"They have provided screenshots."

"Excellent."

"They have provided technical details."

"Excellent."

The Junior Officer looked hopeful.

"So we'll investigate?"

The Officer shook his head.

"That would encourage reporting."

Weeks passed.

Additional reports arrived.

The vulnerability remained.

The Programme Office remained confident.

The dashboard remained green.

Then one morning the issue became public.

The media noticed.

The public noticed.

The Committees noticed.

The Vendor noticed.

The Programme Office suddenly became interested.

An emergency meeting was convened.

The same report was presented.

The same screenshots were displayed.

The same technical details were reviewed.

The Officer looked impressed.

"Remarkable."

The Junior Officer stared.

"It's the same report."

"Yes."

"You ignored it three months ago."

"That was a concern."

The Officer pointed at the projector.

"Today it is a finding."

The vulnerability was eventually fixed.

The reporter received a thank-you email.

The Programme Office issued a statement.

The Vendor issued a statement.

The Committee issued a statement.

Everyone congratulated themselves.

The Junior Officer looked confused.

"Who discovered the problem?"

The Officer thought carefully.

"The Committee."

"The reporter submitted everything months ago."

"Yes."

The Officer smiled.

"The Committee discovered it later."

The Junior Officer made a note.

Timing, apparently, was an important component of expertise.
