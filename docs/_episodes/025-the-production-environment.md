# DOFH #025

## The Production Environment

The system had performed flawlessly.

Every demonstration succeeded.

Every presentation succeeded.

Every review succeeded.

Every dashboard was green.

The Junior Officer entered carrying a new report.

"Sir."

The Digital Officer From Hell looked up.

"The deployment?"

"The deployment."

The Officer smiled.

"Excellent."

"The system is failing."

The Officer frowned.

"That's impossible."

"Why?"

"Because it passed testing."

The Junior Officer opened the report.

"Users are reporting errors."

"Interesting."

"The application crashes."

"Interesting."

"The portal becomes unavailable under load."

The Officer looked surprised.

"There was no load during testing."

The Junior Officer stared.

"What?"

The Officer pointed at the architecture diagram.

"The testing environment was carefully controlled."

"How carefully?"

"No users."

The Junior Officer blinked.

"No users?"

"Of course not."

The Vendor nodded.

The Consultants nodded.

The Programme Office nodded.

The Junior Officer felt increasingly isolated.

"So the first time the system encountered actual users..."

The Officer smiled.

"Was production."

The Junior Officer reviewed the readiness report.

Everything appeared perfect.

The deployment report looked less perfect.

The incident reports looked even less perfect.

By afternoon a meeting had been convened.

The Vendor explained that production differed from testing.

The Consultants explained that reality differed from assumptions.

The Programme Office explained that lessons would be learned.

The Officer finished his tea.

"Remarkable."

"What?"

"The environment appears hostile."

The Junior Officer looked at the incident dashboard.

"The environment is citizens."

The Officer nodded.

"Exactly."

The Junior Officer made a note.

Reality had once again become an unsupported configuration.
