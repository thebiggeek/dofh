# DOFH #027

## The Corrective Action Plan

The Findings had finally been accepted.

The Audit was complete.

The Experts had presented.

The Committees had concurred.

The Consultants had facilitated.

The Vendor had acknowledged.

The Programme Office had welcomed the recommendations.

The Junior Officer entered carrying a document.

"Sir."

The Digital Officer From Hell looked up.

"The Corrective Action Plan?"

"The Corrective Action Plan."

The Officer smiled.

"Excellent."

The document was enormous.

The Findings Report had been three hundred pages.

The Corrective Action Plan was four hundred.

The Officer appeared pleased.

"What does it say?"

The Junior Officer opened the executive summary.

"It says we should improve governance."

"Excellent."

"It says we should strengthen oversight."

"Excellent."

"It says we should enhance accountability."

"Excellent."

"It says we should establish clearer reporting mechanisms."

"Excellent."

The Officer nodded approvingly.

The Junior Officer turned another page.

"It also says we should review certification procedures."

"Excellent."

"Review vendor management."

"Excellent."

"Review pilot programmes."

"Excellent."

"Review risk management."

"Excellent."

The Officer took a sip of tea.

The Junior Officer continued reading.

"It recommends creating a Steering Committee."

"Excellent."

"A Governance Committee."

"Excellent."

"A Risk Committee."

"Excellent."

"A Programme Review Committee."

"Excellent."

The Junior Officer paused.

"Sir."

"Yes?"

"Most of these committees already exist."

The Officer smiled.

"Then implementation will be straightforward."

Several weeks later implementation began.

The Steering Committee met.

The Governance Committee met.

The Risk Committee met.

The Programme Review Committee met.

The Committees produced reports.

The reports recommended coordination.

A Coordination Committee was established.

The Junior Officer looked exhausted.

"Sir."

"Yes?"

"Have we fixed anything?"

The Officer looked puzzled.

"What do you mean?"

"The findings."

"The issues."

"The recommendations."

"The actual problems."

The Officer examined the Corrective Action Plan.

The document contained:

* 147 recommendations
* 83 action items
* 41 review mechanisms
* 19 oversight structures
* 12 reporting frameworks

The Officer looked satisfied.

"It appears so."

The Junior Officer stared.

"Where?"

The Officer pointed to a chart.

IMPLEMENTATION PROGRESS

97%

"That's implementation of the plan."

"Exactly."

"Not implementation of the fixes."

The Officer leaned back.

"Young man."

"Yes sir."

"The purpose of a Corrective Action Plan is not to correct things."

The Junior Officer blinked.

"It isn't?"

The Officer shook his head.

"It is to demonstrate commitment to correction."

The room fell silent.

The Junior Officer reviewed the final section.

SUCCESS CRITERIA

* Plan approved
* Committees established
* Reporting framework operational
* Progress dashboard available

There was no mention of outcomes.

There was no mention of users.

There was no mention of whether anything actually worked.

The Officer signed the approval.

The Committees approved the implementation.

The dashboard turned green.

The Audit was closed.

The Findings were resolved.

The issues remained.

Months later a new review began.

The first recommendation was immediately accepted.

A Corrective Action Plan would be required.

The Officer smiled.

The framework was working perfectly.
