# DOFH #029

## The Transfer

The Findings had been accepted.

The Audit had concluded.

The Corrective Action Plan had been implemented.

The public remained unconvinced.

The Junior Officer entered carrying a newspaper.

"Sir."

The Digital Officer From Hell looked up.

"There appears to be public outrage."

The Officer nodded.

"There usually is."

"This one is demanding accountability."

The Officer sighed.

"Very well."

Several weeks later a committee completed its review.

The report was extensive.

The conclusions were detailed.

The recommendations were unanimous.

Action would be taken.

The newspapers celebrated.

The commentators celebrated.

The public celebrated.

The Junior Officer celebrated.

The Officer appeared calm.

"What happens now?" asked the Junior Officer.

The Officer packed a cardboard box.

"A transfer."

The Junior Officer looked devastated.

"Sir."

"Yes?"

"This is serious."

The Officer nodded.

"It appears so."

The transfer order arrived.

The Officer read it carefully.

Director, Strategic Programme Excellence.

The office was larger.

The budget was larger.

The staff was larger.

The tea allowance was significantly larger.

The Junior Officer looked confused.

"I thought this was punishment."

"It is."

"This looks like a promotion."

The Officer smiled.

"Young man."

"Yes sir."

"Never confuse movement with consequences."

Months later the Junior Officer attended a conference.

A keynote speaker was being introduced.

Distinguished Transformation Leader.

Governance Expert.

Programme Excellence Advisor.

The Officer walked onto the stage.

The audience applauded.

The Junior Officer stared.

The Officer adjusted the microphone.

"Our greatest lesson," he began, "is accountability."

The applause continued.

The Junior Officer stopped taking notes.

Reality clearly had different documentation standards.
