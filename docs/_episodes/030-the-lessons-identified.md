# DOFH #030

## The Lessons Identified

The final meeting of the Transformation Programme had arrived.

The conference room was full.

The Vendor attended.

The Consultants attended.

The Programme Office attended.

The Committees attended.

The Experts attended.

The Auditors attended.

The Junior Officer attended.

The Digital Officer From Hell chaired the meeting.

The final report was presented.

Five hundred and twelve pages.

Twenty-three appendices.

Eight executive summaries.

Three dashboards.

One conclusion.

The Junior Officer raised his hand.

"Sir."

"Yes?"

"What lessons have we learned?"

The room became silent.

The Officer looked puzzled.

"Learned?"

"Yes."

The Officer flipped through the report.

Eventually he found the final chapter.

LESSONS IDENTIFIED

The Junior Officer looked hopeful.

"Excellent."

"What are they?"

The Officer began reading.

"Risks should be monitored."

The Junior Officer nodded.

"Good."

"Warnings should be reviewed."

"Good."

"Pilot findings should be considered."

"Very good."

"Experts should be consulted."

"Excellent."

The Junior Officer waited.

The Officer reached the final page.

"That's it."

The Junior Officer looked confused.

"Those were all known before the programme started."

The Officer smiled.

"Precisely."

The Junior Officer stared.

"So what did we learn?"

The Officer looked offended.

"Young man."

"Yes sir."

"The report says lessons identified."

"Not learned?"

"Learning is outside project scope."

The room nodded in agreement.

The Committees approved the report.

The Auditors accepted the findings.

The Programme Office closed the project.

The dashboard turned green.

The archive process began.

The Junior Officer looked at the final recommendation.

Future programmes should incorporate lessons identified from previous programmes.

He turned to the next folder on the table.

DIGITAL TRANSFORMATION 2.0

The first page read:

PROJECT INITIATION DOCUMENT

The second page read:

LESSONS TO BE IDENTIFIED

The Junior Officer slowly closed the folder.

Across the table, the Officer smiled.

The cycle was ready to begin again.
