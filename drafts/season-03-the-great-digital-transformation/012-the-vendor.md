# DOFH #012

## The Vendor

The Junior Officer entered carrying a procurement report.

"Sir, the bids have been evaluated."

The Digital Officer From Hell nodded.

"Excellent."

"We have a preferred vendor."

The Officer smiled.

"I assume they were the lowest bidder."

"Yes."

"Excellent."

The Junior Officer looked uncomfortable.

"There are some concerns."

The Officer sighed.

"There always are."

"The second bidder has implemented similar systems before."

"Disqualified."

"The third bidder operates systems at national scale."

"Disqualified."

"The fourth bidder demonstrated successful deployments."

"Very suspicious."

The Junior Officer looked down at the report.

"The selected bidder has never built anything comparable."

The Officer nodded.

"Excellent."

The Junior Officer blinked.

"Sir?"

"Fresh perspective."

"They have no experience."

"They have enthusiasm."

"They have no references."

"They have ambition."

"They have no large deployments."

"They have potential."

The Junior Officer looked defeated.

"Why were they selected?"

The Officer pointed at a number.

The lowest number in the entire document.

"That."

"The price?"

"The price."

"But this project is critical."

"Everything is critical."

"It affects millions."

"Everything affects millions."

The Junior Officer sighed.

The Officer leaned back.

"Young man."

"Yes sir."

"Do you know what L1 means?"

"The lowest bidder."

"Incorrect."

The Junior Officer looked confused.

"It means certainty."

"How?"

"The procurement process has certainty."

"What about delivery?"

The Officer ignored the question.

"The forms were complete."

"Yes."

"The signatures were correct."

"Yes."

"The declarations were submitted."

"Yes."

"The stamps were present."

"Yes."

The Officer spread his hands.

"Then procurement has succeeded."

The Junior Officer stared.

"What about the project?"

The Officer smiled.

"That is now the vendor's problem."

Weeks later the contract was signed.

Photographs were taken.

Hands were shaken.

Announcements were made.

The transformation programme moved forward.

The vendor inherited impossible deadlines.

The technical team inherited impossible expectations.

The public inherited future surprises.

The Officer received another cup of tea.

Everyone was progressing.

Some faster than others.
