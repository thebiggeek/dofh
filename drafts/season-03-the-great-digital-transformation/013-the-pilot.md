# DOFH #013

## The Pilot

The Junior Officer entered carrying a risk assessment.

The Digital Officer From Hell immediately became suspicious.

"What's that?"

"A risk assessment."

The Officer frowned.

"Why?"

"Because the transformation programme is approaching deployment."

"Excellent."

"The technical team recommends a pilot."

The Officer stared.

"A what?"

"A pilot."

The Officer looked uncomfortable.

The Junior Officer continued.

"A small-scale deployment."

"Why?"

"To identify problems."

The Officer looked horrified.

"Before launch?"

"Yes."

The room fell silent.

The Officer slowly placed his tea cup on the desk.

"Young man."

"Yes sir."

"What happens if the pilot discovers problems?"

"We fix them."

The Officer looked even more concerned.

"And if there are many problems?"

"We fix those too."

The Officer stood up.

"This is precisely why pilots are dangerous."

The Junior Officer blinked.

"Dangerous?"

"Very."

The Officer walked to the whiteboard.

On it he wrote:

PILOT

↓

DISCOVER PROBLEMS

↓

DELAY LAUNCH

The Junior Officer nodded.

"That seems logical."

The Officer crossed it out.

"Now let me show you the approved process."

He wrote:

GO LIVE

↓

DISCOVER PROBLEMS

↓

FORM COMMITTEE

↓

ISSUE ASSURANCE

The Junior Officer looked at both diagrams.

The second one was significantly longer.

"Why is this better?"

The Officer smiled.

"It creates stakeholder engagement."

The Junior Officer wasn't convinced.

"The system affects millions of users."

"Excellent."

"The risks are substantial."

"Excellent."

"The infrastructure has never operated at this scale."

"Excellent."

The Junior Officer looked down at the report.

"The technical team strongly recommends a pilot."

The Officer took the report.

He stamped it.

NOTED

Then he filed it.

"Will we conduct a pilot?"

"Certainly."

The Junior Officer looked relieved.

"When?"

The Officer smiled.

"Immediately after launch."

The Junior Officer stared.

"That's not a pilot."

The Officer leaned back.

"It is."

"How?"

The Officer pointed out the window.

At the city.

At the offices.

At the homes.

At the millions of future users.

"The entire country," he said, "will participate."
