# DOFH #024

## The Dry Run

The Pilot had been declared a success.

The Digital Officer From Hell was therefore surprised to learn that people were treating it as a warning.

The Junior Officer entered carrying two thick reports.

"Sir."

The Officer looked up.

"The Pilot?"

"The Pilot."

The Officer smiled.

"Excellent. How did it go?"

The Junior Officer opened the first report.

"The pilot identified capacity issues."

"Excellent."

"The pilot identified process issues."

"Excellent."

"The pilot identified security issues."

"Excellent."

"The pilot identified operational issues."

The Officer nodded approvingly.

"A very productive pilot."

The Junior Officer looked confused.

"The report recommends delaying deployment."

The Officer frowned.

"Why?"

"To address the issues."

The Officer looked horrified.

"Before launch?"

"Yes."

The Officer placed his tea down carefully.

The room fell silent.

The Vendor looked uncomfortable.

The Consultants looked interested.

The Programme Office looked elsewhere.

The Junior Officer continued.

"The second report reaches the same conclusion."

"Interesting."

"It recommends postponing deployment."

The Officer examined the schedule.

Launch Date.

Launch Ceremony.

Press Briefing.

Success Announcement.

All already booked.

"This is unfortunate."

"What is?"

"The reports arrived after the invitations."

The Junior Officer blinked.

"The reports arrived before launch."

"Exactly."

The Officer leaned back.

"Far too late."

Weeks later the deployment proceeded exactly as scheduled.

The same issues appeared.

The same warnings proved accurate.

The same recommendations became findings.

The Junior Officer reviewed the pilot reports.

Everything had been predicted.

Everything had been documented.

Everything had been acknowledged.

"Sir."

"Yes?"

"Why did we conduct the pilot?"

The Officer smiled.

"Governance."

"And why did we ignore it?"

The Officer looked puzzled.

"Young man."

"Yes sir."

"The purpose of a dry run is to identify problems."

"Not fix them?"

The Officer shook his head.

"That would expand the scope."
