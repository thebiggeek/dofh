# DOFH #026

## The Findings

Six months after the Transformation Programme entered production, the Findings were finally presented.

The conference room was full.

The Vendor attended.

The Consultants attended.

The Programme Office attended.

The Committees attended.

The Experts attended.

The Junior Officer attended.

The Digital Officer From Hell chaired the meeting.

The lead auditor stood.

"After extensive review, we have reached our conclusions."

The Officer nodded.

"Excellent."

The auditor opened the report.

"The deployment suffered from capacity issues."

The Junior Officer looked surprised.

The Officer did not.

The auditor continued.

"The deployment suffered from governance issues."

The Officer nodded.

"Excellent."

"The deployment suffered from security issues."

"Excellent."

"The deployment suffered from operational issues."

"Excellent."

The Junior Officer frowned.

"Sir."

"Yes?"

"Those are the same issues identified in the pilot."

The Officer nodded.

The auditor turned another page.

"The findings are also consistent with the readiness review."

The Junior Officer looked confused.

"Those were identified before launch."

"Yes."

The auditor continued.

"The findings are also consistent with the technical assessment."

The Junior Officer looked increasingly concerned.

"That was identified before launch too."

"Yes."

The auditor continued.

"The findings are also consistent with the risk assessment."

The Junior Officer stared.

"Before launch?"

"Before launch."

The auditor nodded.

"The findings are also consistent with reports submitted by independent researchers."

The Junior Officer slowly lowered his notebook.

The room became quiet.

The auditor reached the conclusion section.

"Therefore, we conclude that the warning signs were visible."

The Officer smiled.

"A valuable insight."

The Junior Officer looked at the report.

Every major finding already existed in earlier reports.

Every recommendation already existed.

Every warning already existed.

The only new thing was the cover page.

"Sir."

"Yes?"

"What exactly did the audit discover?"

The Officer considered the question.

"The audit discovered that the previous reports were correct."

The Junior Officer blinked.

"It took six months to discover that?"

The Officer looked puzzled.

"Young man."

"Yes sir."

"Verification is an important governance function."

The auditor continued.

"Future warnings should be reviewed carefully."

The recommendation was unanimously accepted.

A committee was immediately formed.

Its purpose would be to review future warnings.

The Junior Officer looked around the room.

"Will anything change?"

The Officer smiled.

"Certainly."

"What?"

The Officer pointed at the report.

The title read:

FINDINGS

The previous report had been titled:

CONCERNS

The Officer leaned back.

"That is significant progress."

The meeting concluded.

The report was approved.

The findings were accepted.

The lessons were identified.

The next transformation programme entered planning.
