# Procurement Terminology

## Tender

A process through which the government attempts to purchase something.

Often results in purchasing something else.

---

## L1

Lowest Bidder.

Traditionally considered the most qualified entity in the universe.

---

## L2

The bidder who later explains why L1 was a bad idea.

---

## L3

Usually correct.

Never selected.

---

## Technical Evaluation

The process of ensuring unwanted vendors are removed before financial bids are opened.

---

## Financial Evaluation

The process of discovering why technical evaluation was performed.

---

## Disqualified

A state achieved when a bidder fails to satisfy a requirement.

Examples include:

* Missing signature
* Missing stamp
* Wrong stamp
* Blue ink instead of black ink
* Excess competence

---

## Corrigendum

A mechanism used to modify requirements after publication.

Additional corrigenda may be issued to explain previous corrigenda.

---

## Scope of Work

A fictional document intended to describe what will be delivered.

---

## Change Request

Evidence that the Scope of Work was fictional.

---

## Emergency Procurement

Standard procurement conducted at higher speed and greater cost.

---

## Single Vendor Situation

A condition discovered immediately after all other vendors are disqualified.

---

## Project Completion

The point at which the project enters maintenance mode.

Maintenance mode may continue indefinitely.
